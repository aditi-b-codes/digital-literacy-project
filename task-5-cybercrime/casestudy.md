# UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud is a rapidly increasing form of cybercrime in India, where attackers trick users into transferring money through digital payment platforms such as Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. These scams often rely on social engineering techniques to manipulate victims into taking actions that result in financial loss.

In a typical scenario, a college student posts an item for sale on an online marketplace. Shortly after, they receive a call or message from a person posing as an interested buyer. The fraudster agrees to purchase the item and claims to send the payment via UPI. To “receive” the money, the victim is asked to scan a QR code or approve a payment request. Trusting the buyer, the student follows the instructions and enters their UPI PIN, unknowingly authorizing a debit transaction instead of receiving money.

Such frauds commonly target students, first-time digital payment users, and individuals who are not fully aware of how UPI systems work. The attackers exploit urgency, trust, and lack of technical knowledge to carry out the scam successfully.

The consequences of such incidents can be severe. Victims may lose significant amounts of money within minutes, and recovery is often difficult due to the speed of transactions. Additionally, victims may experience stress, loss of confidence, and hesitation in using digital platforms in the future.

This case highlights the importance of digital awareness, careful verification, and responsible use of online payment systems.
